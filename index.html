Race to the White House: Recreating the map of the US 2020 elections results

By Richard A. Abbey

The maps below detail how the Democrats swept through the country to win the race to the White House in the keenly contested US Presidential elections in 2020.

The first map is a chloropeth map which provides a breakdown of electoral results between the two leading candidates, Joseph Biden and Donald Trump.

The map shows results at two levels, county and state. By zooming into the first map, the details reveal how the elections played out at the county level.

The map's default position, however, shows each party's performance at the state level.

The second map is an interactive graduated points map. The essence of this type of map is to use bigger circles to the magnitude of votes.

This map uses county-level data to highlight the numeric difference in performance of the two candidates.

While the red dots seem widspread, the blue dots were often far bigger.
